To the 87,013 Brides Who Married Today

Ronan Rahl

In Abilene an uncle walks his
brother's daughter across a pasture
still patchy from the herd.
An abuela in Savannah pins a veil
gone ivory. Her hands know where it sits.
She wore it too.
Alison today gets the Garden,
no mud on her red soles,
no borrowed lace, just crisp white
and the blue glare of a million screens—
a whole country watching her do it.
Along the harbor in Boston, I hold
one of those screens the way I have
since before thirteen, singing every word back
to a woman who has never heard me.
Tonight the sky lights up
for all eighty-seven thousand
and thirteen of you.
You said it today.
You never asked me to.
I do.
