<div class="stanza">
In Abilene an uncle walks his<br>
brother's daughter across a pasture<br>
still patchy from the herd.<br>
An abuela in Savannah pins a veil<br>
gone ivory. Her hands know where it sits.<br>
She wore it too.
</div>

<div class="stanza">
Alison today gets the Garden,<br>
no mud on her red soles,<br>
no borrowed lace, just crisp white<br>
and the blue glare of a million screens—<br>
a whole country watching her do it.
</div>

<div class="stanza">
Along the harbor in Boston, I hold<br>
one of those screens the way I have<br>
since before thirteen, singing every word back<br>
to a woman who has never heard me.
</div>

<div class="stanza">
Tonight the sky lights up<br>
for all eighty-seven thousand<br>
and thirteen of you.
</div>

<div class="stanza">
You said it today.<br>
You never asked me to.
</div>

<div class="stanza">
I do.
</div>
