<div class="stanza">"And what if time travel already existed?"<br>The kind where you go back<br>to a <em>when.</em><br>I pondered one winter night before a nor'easter.</div>

<div class="stanza">The first time I listen to a song—<br>It is geography. It is weather.<br>It is the people I knew then,<br>and the people I know now.</div>

<div class="stanza"><em>White horse.</em><br>This kid in the backseat of a car,<br>his red iPod gripped tight,<br>pausing Eminem on loop, for her—<br>looks up in the rearview mirror,<br>catches himself at twenty-seven,<br>just thriving.</div>

<div class="stanza"><em>Maroon.</em><br>That long walk under<br>the midnight blue, with you—<br>it stays where we left it,<br>third from the top of the playlist.<br>Part of my morning ritual now<br>before the tea steeps, just me.</div>

<div class="stanza"><em>Ophelia.</em><br>Early October, kitchen lights<br>left on, mom and dad right here.<br>Let me take you behind<br>the curtain of my life—<br>a whole lot of nothing<br>is somehow my everything.</div>

<div class="stanza">The power went out that night.<br>I pressed play—<br>ready for the next <em>when.</em></div>
